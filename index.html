Mission Statement.

Open source practices benefit education, advocacy, development, and involvement in the computer and technology communities. We apply these practices in the building of practice and creative projects and applications.

GitHub

Club Uniqueness

BUGS is focused on the promotion of open source practices through the engagement of short and long term projects, both practical and creative. These projects may be student, faculty, or outside expert led, with explanation of how open source has an impact in fields and subjects relevant to the project.

Constituency

We are aimed at attracting all students who have a passion to learn and engage in projects within the computer and technology fields.

Opportunities

Open source is a philosophy of sharing and transparency for the development of applications and projects, allowing for open inspection, modification, and enhancement. Anyone who uses the internet today is directly interacting with the products of open source initiatives, from Apache Web server to the entire operating system of Linux.

Plan

Our club will employ projects to teach skills and strategies that will be applicable to many fields, from computers to biology and chemistry. These projects may include a faculty member or outside expert from the respective field to further promote the usefulness of the skill being taught.

Traction

The club has founding members in computer science and natural science majors within NYU. These members work with faculty members on open source applications within NYU labs from various fields. These applications within various fields can be taught and employed within the club.
